Shri Vrinda Devi Temple

Vrindavan is completely under Vrinda-devi’s grace. Without the mercy of Srimati Vrinda-devi, it is not possible to enter into the pastimes of Srimati Radharani and Krishna, not even as a listener in an audience.
District: Mathura
State: Uttarpradesh

Overview

The whole Braj is the home of Vrinda Devi. It is written in scriptures about this place, that over one and a half miles distance from Nandgaon on the west side, you will find Vrinda Devi's Residence. So this is the same Vrinda Devi's place, a distance of one and a half-mile, situated in the west direction of Nandgaon. On the front side is the Vrinda Kund and on the backside, there is Gupt Kund, the first yoga peeth. There are a total of 8 yoga peeth's in Braj where Radha and Krishna perform various pastimes. The first of these is the Gupt Kund situated just backside of this temple. The queen of Braj is Shri Radha and the king is Shri Krishna and the pastimes are managed by Vrinda Devi. Every pastime in Braj is headed by Vrinda Devi. From 7:00 to 8:24 in the morning there are still pastimes going on. Everywhere Mangala Aarti starts from 4:00 am but here at 7:00 in the morning, but this temple opens at 7:00 am When the temple opens at 7:00 morning, Radha Krishna enters in pastimes, Vrinda Devi comes here to manage it. The temple opens after Radha Krishna arrives, Pastimes starts from 7:00 and ends at 8:24 morning, Radha Krishna goes after finishing Pastimes. Krishna goes for cow grazing and Radha Rani goes to worship the sun god. When you go from Chhata to Govardhana, there is a village named Bhanakala, Radha Rani goes there to worships sun god.

Interesting Facts about this temple

Vrindavana Forest: Sacred Legends and Significance of Vrinda-devi's Penance

The Vrindavana forest is named after Vrinda-devi, who performed penances here for 60,000 years. She was born as the daughter of Emperor Kedera, a very pious king that attained Goloka Vrindavana. His daughter Vrinda-devi, after sanctifying the forest by her penance, also attained Goloka.

Aesthetic place to visit

The divine kund is lined by beautiful flower gardens and trees that significantly add to the beauty of the place. Also known as the Tulsi, Vrinda Devi is believed to be the presiding goddess of Vrindavan and hence is worshipped here since the ancient times. It is believed that no visit to the Braj dham is complete without the visit to this pious temple. The beautiful stone carved temple in the woods is the abode of goddess Vrinda. Lying between the two kunds, Gupt and Vrinda Kund the exquisite Vrinda Devi Temple is also said to be the cultural heritage of the Mathura region.

Preserving the Legacy: The Story of Vraja's Disfigured Deities and Their Continued Presence

Some five thousand years ago, after Krishna and His associates wound up Their earthly pastimes, the Lord's great-grandson, Vajranabha Maharaja, installed Deities of Krishna all over Vraja. Deities were installed also at Vrinda Kunda. Later, unfortunately, They were disfigured by Muslim invaders and for some time They disappeared from human sight. Today, although They are no longer worshiped, They are still giving darshan in a small kutir by the side of the main temple, under an old chokara tree.
